## Existing Projects

### 1. Planet Hunters (Zooniverse)

**What it is:**
- Citizen science project on Zooniverse platform
- Users manually analyze light curves from Kepler and TESS
- Look for transit signals by eye
- Launched by Yale University (Debra Fischer)
- Multiple versions: Planet Hunters, Planet Hunters 2.0, Planet Hunters TESS (PHT)

**Key Features:**
- Human classification of light curves
- Focus on transit detection
- Community discussion boards
- Published scientific papers from discoveries
- Integration with ExoFOP for candidate submission

**Limitations:**
- **Manual classification only** - no automated pipeline
- **No K Dwarf focus** - searches all star types
- **No habitability scoring** - just detection
- **No user accounts/storage** - no personal workspace
- **No social features** - basic discussion only
- **No coordinate selection** - users work on pre-selected data
- **No results saving** - contributions go to researchers, not personal archive
- **No two-list system** - all data mixed together
- **No premium tiers** - completely free, no sustainability model

**Target Audience:**
- General public interested in astronomy
- No specialized knowledge required
- Casual participation

---

### 2. ExoFOP (Exoplanet Follow-up Observing Program)

**What it is:**
- NASA's platform for exoplanet candidate follow-up
- Professional astronomy community collaboration
- Coordinates follow-up observations
- Manages candidate status (TOI, CTOI, etc.)
- Run by NASA's Exoplanet Science Institute (NExScI)

**Key Features:**
- Professional-grade data management
- Follow-up observation coordination
- Community consensus on candidate validation
- Integration with major surveys (Kepler, K2, TESS)
- 1 million user-submitted files milestone

**Limitations:**
- **Professional-focused** - not citizen-friendly
- **No automated pipeline** - requires professional expertise
- **No K Dwarf focus** - handles all exoplanets
- **No habitability scoring** - technical parameters only
- **No social features** - pure collaboration tool
- **No user personal workspace** - institutional accounts only
- **No two-list system** - all candidates mixed
- **No premium tiers** - NASA-funded

**Target Audience:**
- Professional astronomers
- Research institutions
- Graduate students

## ExoQ - Unique Differentiators

### 1. **K Dwarf-Focused Platform**

**Differentiation:**
- **Only platform specifically targeting K Dwarf stars**
- All features optimized for K Dwarf characteristics
- Pre-filtered coordinate lists (vetted + virgin)
- K Dwarf-specific habitability scoring
- Educational content about K Dwarf advantages

**Why This Matters:**
- K Dwarfs are scientifically the best targets for Earth 2.0
- Focus increases discovery probability
- Niche positioning creates clear identity
- Avoids competition with general-purpose platforms

---

### 2. **Two-List System (Vetted vs Virgin)**

**Differentiation:**
- **Vetted K Dwarfs** - ready for exoplanet vetting (quick wins)
- **Virgin Coordinates** - untouched stars for new discovery (exploration)
- **Clear user choice** - based on experience level and goals
- **12-star limit** - manageable pipeline feeds
- **Progressive difficulty** - start easy, advance to discovery

**Why This Matters:**
- Lowers barrier to entry (vetted list for beginners)
- Provides clear path (vetted → virgin)
- Gamifies the experience (unlock new challenges)
- No other platform offers this structure

---

### 3. **Automated Pipeline ExoQ + User Choice**

**Differentiation:**
- **Automated scientific pipeline** - not manual classification
- **User selects coordinates** - not pre-assigned data
- **Two pipeline types** - vetting vs discovery
- **Quality-controlled output** - peer-review ready
- **User controls the search** - agency and ownership

**Why This Matters:**
- Professional-grade results without professional expertise
- Users feel ownership of discoveries
- Scalable beyond manual classification

- Reproducible and scientifically rigorous

## Unique Value Proposition (UVP)

**ExoQ is the only community-driven platform that:**

1. **Focuses exclusively on K Dwarf stars** — the most promising targets for Earth 2.0
2. **Provides two starting paths** — vetted candidates for quick wins, virgin coordinates for new discovery
3. **Automates the scientific pipeline** — professional-grade analysis without requiring expertise
4. **Offers personal workspace with storage** — users build their own scientific portfolio
5. **Features social community gallery** — share discoveries with likes, comments, and social sharing
6. **Uses storage-based premium model** — sustainable operations while keeping core features free
7. **Anchors on Earth 2.0 mission** — emotional connection to saving humanity

**In one sentence:**
"ExoQ is the only K Dwarf-focused, community-powered exoplanet discovery platform that combines automated scientific pipelines with personal workspaces and social features, making the search for Earth 2.0 accessible, engaging, and sustainable."

## Recommendations for Sharp Differentiation

### 1. **Lean Hard into K Dwarf Focus**
- All branding emphasizes K Dwarfs
